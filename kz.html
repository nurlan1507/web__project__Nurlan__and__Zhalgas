Beshbarmak

The hallmark of a feast in Kazakhstan is beshbarmak. This is a very ancient dish, consisting of pieces of boiled lamb, broth and pieces of dough. In translation, "beshbarmak" means "five fingers", because they eat it with their hands. 1.5 - 2 kg. lamb meat (you can take beef), 4 onions, 2 carrots, parsley (parsnip) roots, salt, peppercorns, 100 ml. 3% vinegar (you can take apple cider vinegar). For the dough: 1 egg, 3 cups flour, 250 ml. broth

kuyrdak

When you go to a restaurant of national Kazakh cuisine, ask them to bring you kuyrdak - roast meat, lots of onions and vegetables. The name of the dish comes from "kuyru" (to fry). This, in fact, is the recipe. Offal is used from meat (liver, kidneys, heart, lungs). From vegetables - traditional potatoes, carrots, onions, pumpkins. lungs - 500 g, lamb heart - 200 g, onions - 2 pcs., black pepper - 1 pinch, garlic - 1 cup, potatoes - 500 g, greens - to taste bay leaf - to taste

Moti

Kazakh cuisine is also manti: a dish of finely chopped meat and thinly rolled dough, which is steamed. There are several recipes for making manti, and the secrets of originality are in minced meat. Today, every Kazakh housewife has her own version of the national dish. By the way, in the translation "manti" means "stuffed head" ("head of a barbarian"). for the dough: flour 300g., salt 1 tsp, warm water. for the filling: ground beef 500g., onion 4pcs., salt 1\2tsp, spice for minced meat, ground black and red peppers
